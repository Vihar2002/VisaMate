# SCHENGEN AREA  —  STUDENT VISA  (National Long-Stay Visa, Type D)
# Source Consolidation Date: 2025-04-10
# Primary Sources:
#   • Regulation (EU) 2016/801 – Students & Researchers Directive
#   • European Migration Network “Study Visas in the EU” 2024 report
#   • Embassy checklists of Germany, France, Spain (updated Mar 2025)

SECTION 1 · PURPOSE & VALIDITY
The National Long-Stay “D” Visa allows a third-country national to reside in a Schengen Member State
for a **course or research programme longer than 90 days**.  
Holders may receive a residence permit on arrival and travel freely within the
Schengen Area for up to 90 days per 180-day period.

SECTION 2 · CORE DOCUMENT CHECKLIST
1. **Passport** valid ≥ 3 months beyond programme end, issued ≤ 10 years ago, ≥ 2 blank pages.  
2. **Visa application form** (original + 1 copy), fully completed and signed.  
3. **Recent photograph** 35 mm × 45 mm (white background, neutral face).  
4. **Letter of admission / enrolment** from an accredited university or higher-education institution, stating:  
   • course title and duration,  • tuition fees,  • language of instruction.  
5. **Proof of sufficient financial means** for tuition and living costs:  
   • Bank statement, scholarship letter, or formal sponsor affidavit.  
   • Minimum guideline: **€840–€1 200 per month** (exact figure set by host state).  
6. **Proof of accommodation** (dorm contract, rental lease, or host declaration).  
7. **Comprehensive health insurance** valid in Schengen, minimum cover **€30 000**.  
8. **Academic transcripts & diplomas** (legalised and, if required, translated).  
9. **Motivation letter** outlining study goals and future plans.  
10. **Receipt of visa fee payment** (see Section 4).

SECTION 3 · OPTIONAL / COUNTRY-SPECIFIC DOCUMENTS
• Police clearance certificate (for stays > 6 months, many states).  
• Language-proficiency certificate (B1/B2) if programme not in English.  
• Blocked account confirmation (e.g., Germany “Sperrkonto” €11 208 for first year).  
• Birth certificate + parental consent if applicant < 18 years.

SECTION 4 · FEES
• Standard National Student Visa fee: **€90** for adults.  
• Children (6–12 yrs): **€45**.  
Some Member States waive the fee for EU-funded scholarship holders (e.g., Erasmus +).

SECTION 5 · APPLICATION TIMELINE
1. **Book appointment** with the consulate or visa-application centre of the country of main study.  
2. Earliest submission: **6 months** before intended entry; latest: **15 days** before.  
3. Attend appointment, submit biometrics (fingerprints & photo) and originals.  
4. Average processing time: **15 calendar days** (can extend to 45 days for complex cases).  
5. Residence-permit card must be collected in host country within 90 days of arrival.

SECTION 6 · AFTER ARRIVAL
• Register address with local municipality (within 3 – 14 days, varies by state).  
• Apply for student residence permit if the D-visa is issued as an entry visa only.  
• Permitted work: up to **20 h/week** during semester and full-time in vacations (check host-country caps).

SECTION 7 · FAMILY MEMBERS
Family (spouse, minor children) **must apply separately** for family-reunification visas; proof of extra funds and health insurance is required.

SECTION 8 · COMMON REFUSAL REASONS
• Financial means below statutory minimum or funds held < 3 months.  
• Admission letter lacking signature or accreditation proof.  
• Missing legalised translations of diplomas.  
• Unclear study plan or suspected intent to overstay.
