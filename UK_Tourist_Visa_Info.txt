# UNITED KINGDOM  —  STANDARD VISITOR VISA  (Tourist / Family / Short Course)
# Source Consolidation Date: 2025-04-10
# Primary Sources:
#   • GOV.UK “Visit the UK as a Standard Visitor”  (March 2025)
#   • UKVI Caseworker Guidance – Visit Visa v9.0  (Feb 2025)
#   • Immigration Rules Appendix V: Visitor  (Jan 2025)
# ==============================================================

SECTION 1 · PURPOSE & VALIDITY
The Standard Visitor Visa allows entry to the UK for **tourism, family visits, business meetings, or short courses** of up to 30 days.  
Maximum stay per visit: **180 days (6 months)**. The visa does **not** permit work (paid or unpaid) or access to public funds.

SECTION 2 · DOCUMENT CHECKLIST
1. **Online application form** (gov.uk) fully completed and submitted.  
2. **Passport** valid for the entire stay with at least one blank page.  
3. **Financial proof**: recent 6-month bank statements or payslips showing the applicant can cover travel, accommodation, and living costs without working or public funds.  
4. **Travel itinerary** and evidence of onward/return travel (reservation or e-ticket).  
5. **Accommodation proof**: hotel booking **or** invitation letter + proof of address from UK host.  
6. If sponsored: signed sponsorship letter and sponsor’s financial evidence.  
7. **TB test certificate** (if resident of a country on UKVI TB list and staying > 6 months).  
8. Additional documents to prove **strong ties** to country of residence (employment letter, property deeds, or enrolment letter).

SECTION 3 · FEES
• **Visa fee**: **£115** (single entry, up to 6 months).  
• Priority visa (optional): £500 (5-day) · Super priority: £1 000 (next-day) — availability varies by VAC.

SECTION 4 · APPLICATION TIMELINE
1. Apply **earliest** 3 months before travel.  
2. Submit online form and pay fees.  
3. Upload documents or attend Visa Application Centre (VAC) for scanning.  
4. Attend **biometric appointment** (fingerprints + digital photograph).  
5. Average processing time: **3 weeks** (standard). Priority services may reduce this to 1 week or 24 h.

SECTION 5 · CONDITIONS & RESTRICTIONS
• **No work** or paid/unpaid internships.  
• Study permitted only if the course ≤ 30 days and incidental to visit.  
• No recourse to public funds, no marriage or civil-partnership notice.  
• Visitors must leave the UK on or before the visa expiry date; overstays jeopardise future applications.

SECTION 6 · ACTIVITIES ALLOWED
✔ Tourism and leisure  
✔ Visiting family and friends  
✔ Business meetings, conferences, signing contracts  
✔ Short recreational courses (≤ 30 days)  
✔ Voluntary work ≤ 30 days for registered UK charity (unpaid, incidental)

SECTION 7 · COMMON REFUSAL REASONS
• Insufficient financial evidence or unexplained large deposits.  
• Weak evidence of intention to return (e.g., no job or study ties).  
• Incomplete itinerary or accommodation proof.  
• Prior UK immigration breaches or overstays.

SECTION 8 · MULTIPLE-ENTRY & LONG-TERM VISITOR OPTIONS
Frequent travellers may apply for a 2-, 5-, or 10-year multiple-entry visitor visa.  
Fee: £400 (2-year) · £771 (5-year) · £963 (10-year). Each visit still capped at 180 days.
