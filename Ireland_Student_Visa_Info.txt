# IRELAND  —  STUDENT VISA  (Long-Stay “D” Visa)
# Source Consolidation Date: 2025-04-10
# Primary Sources:
#   • Irish Naturalisation & Immigration Service (INIS) – “Study Visa” Checklist  (Feb 2025)
#   • Department of Justice – Immigration Policy on Non-EEA Students  (Jan 2025)
#   • DFA “Moving to Ireland for Study” FAQ  (Mar 2025)
# ==============================================================


SECTION 1 · PURPOSE & VALIDITY
A Long-Stay “D” Visa allows a non-EEA national to enter Ireland to pursue
a full-time course of study of **≥ 15 hours per week** that leads to a recognised
qualification (NFQ Level 6 or higher).  
Visa holders receive a **Stamp 2** residence permission on arrival and
may remain for the duration of the programme, renewable annually.

SECTION 2 · CORE DOCUMENT CHECKLIST
1. **Passport** valid for at least **12 months** after proposed date of arrival,
   containing ≥ 2 blank pages.  
2. **Two colour photographs** (45 mm × 35 mm) with applicant’s name on reverse.  
3. **Letter of Acceptance** from an accredited Irish institution confirming:
   • course title and level,  
   • duration and weekly contact hours (≥ 15 h),  
   • tuition fees,  
   • language of instruction.  
4. **Proof of payment** of at least €6 000 tuition deposit **or** full fee receipt.  
5. **Private medical insurance** valid in Ireland (€25 000 minimum medical cover).  
6. **Proof of financial support**:  
   • Personal or sponsor bank statement showing **≥ €10 000** for living expenses
     *plus* any outstanding tuition balance, held for ≥ 6 weeks;  
   • OR official scholarship / sponsorship letter.  
7. **Academic transcripts & English proficiency**: final diploma, transcripts, IELTS
   5.0 + (or TOEFL / Duolingo equivalent).  
8. **Personal statement** detailing study plan and career objectives.  
9. **Evidence of previous immigration compliance** (if applicable).  
10. **Visa fee payment receipt** (see Section 4).

SECTION 3 · ADDITIONAL / CONDITIONAL DOCUMENTS
• **Biometrics** (fingerprints and photo) for certain nationals.  
• **Police Clearance Certificate** if over 18 and studying > 1 year.  
• **Parental consent + birth cert** for applicants under 18.  
• Proof of accommodation (lease, dorm booking) may be requested.

SECTION 4 · FEES
• Visa application fee: **€60** (single-entry) · **€100** (multi-entry).  
• GNIB/IRP card on arrival: €300 per year (paid in Ireland).

SECTION 5 · APPLICATION TIMELINE
1. **Online AVATS application** opened **up to 3 months** before course start.  
2. Pay visa fee and book VFS/embassy appointment.  
3. Submit passport and documents to the designated visa office.  
4. Provide biometric data where required.  
5. Average decision time: **6–8 weeks** (can extend in peak season).  
6. On arrival: register with Garda National Immigration Bureau (GNIB)
   within 30 days.

SECTION 6 · WORK & STUDY CONDITIONS
• Work allowed **20 hours/week** during term; **40 hours/week** 1 Jun–30 Sep
  and 15 Dec–15 Jan.  
• Attendance below 85 % may lead to revocation of Stamp 2.  
• No access to public funds; must maintain private medical insurance.

SECTION 7 · COMMON REFUSAL REASONS
• Funds below €10 000 or large unexplained deposits.  
• Tuition payment not verified.  
• Letter of acceptance from non-accredited college.  
• Inadequate English-language proof.  
• Previous visa over-stay or inconsistent travel history.

SECTION 8 · SPECIAL NOTES
• All previous visa refusals (Irish or otherwise) **must** be disclosed.  
• Applicants under 18 undergo **extra vetting** and must have an appointed guardian in Ireland.  
• Visa decisions can be appealed within 2 months at no extra fee.
