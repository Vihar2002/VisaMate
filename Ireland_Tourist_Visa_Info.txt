# ==============================================================
# IRELAND  —  TOURIST / VISIT VISA   (Short-Stay “C” Visa)
# Source Consolidation Date: 2025-04-10
# Primary Sources:  INIS Tourist Visa Checklist • DFA Travel Advice • Irish
#                   Statutory Instrument S.I. 473/2014 (Fees) • INIS FAQ 2025
# ==============================================================


SECTION 1 · PURPOSE & VALIDITY
A Short-Stay “C” Visa permits visits **up to 90 consecutive days** for tourism, family visits, or short business meetings. The visa is single- or multiple-entry but **cannot be extended in Ireland**. Employment (paid or unpaid) is strictly prohibited.

SECTION 2 · DOCUMENT CHECKLIST (MANDATORY)
1. Passport valid for **≥ 6 months** beyond planned departure from Ireland.
2. Two colour photographs **45 mm × 35 mm**, white background, applicant’s name on reverse.
3. Completed **Visa Application Form AVATS** print-out, signed and dated.
4. **Travel itinerary**: round-trip flight reservation (no ticket purchase required until visa issued).
5. **Proof of accommodation** for entire stay:  
   · Hotel booking **or**  
   · Invitation letter + recent utility bill from private host (must show Eircode).
6. **Bank statements** for the last 6 months showing ≥ €500 – €700 per month of stay.  
   • If a sponsor pays, include sponsor letter + sponsor’s bank statements.
7. **Travel & medical insurance** covering the Schengen area/Ireland with minimum emergency cover of **€30 000**.
8. **Proof of ties to home country**: employment letter, student enrolment letter, or property deeds.
9. Visa fee payment receipt (see Section 4).
10. If under 18: notarised parental consent + birth certificate copy.

SECTION 3 · OPTIONAL SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS
• Letter explaining trip purpose and day-by-day plan.  
• Evidence of previous travel (visas / entry stamps) to demonstrate compliance history.  
• Proof of sufficient cash (€50 per travel day) if bank balance is borderline.  
• Travel history print-out (if travelling from a Schengen country into Ireland).

SECTION 4 · FEES (Statutory Instrument 473/2014)
• Single-entry visa — **€60**  
• Multiple-entry visa — **€100**  
• Transit visa — **€25**  
Fees are non-refundable once the application is lodged.

SECTION 5 · BIOMETRICS & PROCESSING TIME
Fingerprints (biometrics) are collected at the Irish Visa Application Centre or embassy.  
Standard processing time: **6–8 weeks** after biometrics and complete documentation are received.  
Express service is **not** available.

SECTION 6 · CONDITIONS & ENTRY RULES
• Over-stay may result in deportation or a multi-year re-entry ban.  
• The visa does **not** guarantee entry; Immigration Officers can refuse admission.  
• No recourse to public funds; private health cover is mandatory.  
• Visitors must exit Ireland on or before the visa expiry date.

SECTION 7 · EXEMPTIONS & SPECIAL CASES
• Nationals of EEA countries, Switzerland, and the UK are visa-exempt.  
• Holders of a **short-stay visa waiver** (e.g., UK short-stay visa with BIVS notation) may enter Ireland without a separate Irish visa.  
• Certain nationals (e.g., Argentina, Brazil, Japan, South Korea, USA) do not require a tourist visa for visits < 90 days. See INIS “Visa Waiver Programme” list.

SECTION 8 · COMMON REASONS FOR REFUSAL
• Insufficient bank balance or unexplained large credits.  
• Missing travel insurance or proof of accommodation.  
• Doubt over intention to return (weak ties to home country).  
• Inconsistent travel history or fraudulent documents.
